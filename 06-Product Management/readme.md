## 6. Product Managers in Agile & Scrum  
### *How PMs guide teams, shape product direction, and keep delivery on track*
 
Think of the PM as the person who keeps the team focused on solving the *right problems* at the *right time* â€” while making sure the work aligns with user needs and business goals.

In Scrum, the PM often overlaps with the **Product Owner** role, but the core responsibilities remain the same: **define value, prioritize work, guide the team, and validate outcomes.**

>The **Scrum Product Owner (PO)**  
Focuses on the dayâ€‘toâ€‘day execution inside the development team.
Owns the product backlog.
Writes/refines stories, clarifies details, and accepts sprint work.
Ensures the team builds the right thing each sprint.

Scrum is an *iterative* framework. Instead of building everything at once, teams work in short cycles called **sprints**, each producing a usable piece of the product.

#### **A PM ensures that:**

- The team works on the **highest-value features**
- Requirements are clear and achievable
- Work matches both **user needs** and **business strategy**
- Each sprint delivers something meaningful

### The PMâ€™s Core Responsibilities in Scrum

#### 1. **Own and Manage the Product Backlog**
The backlog is the *master list* of all ideas, tasks, and features.

#### PM responsibilities:

- Write user stories  
- Prioritize items (highest value first)  
- Break large ideas into achievable tasks  
- Clarify acceptance criteria  
- Continuously refine backlog items  

**Big Picture:**  
The PM ensures the team is always working on the *right thing* â€” not just what is loudest or most urgent.

#### 2. **Define the Product Vision & Roadmap**
The PM communicates:

- Why the product exists  
- Who itâ€™s for  
- What problems it solves  
- What the future looks like  

**Analogy:**  
The PM is the *navigator* â€” engineering is the *driver*.

#### 3. **Guide Sprint Planning**
During Sprint Planning, the PM:

- Presents high-priority backlog items  
- Explains context, requirements, constraints  
- Answers questions from engineering  
- Ensures the team understands *what success looks like*  
- Helps define a realistic sprint goal  

**The PM does NOT:** assign tasks or decide technical implementation.

#### 4. **Collaborate Daily With the Team**
PMs often join or review standup notes.

Day-to-day duties:

- Provide clarification  
- Unblock decisions  
- Coordinate with stakeholders  
- Confirm priorities  

They ensure the team stays aligned and moving forward.

#### 5. **Review Sprint Work & Accept Features**
During the **Sprint Review**, the PM:

- Looks at completed work  
- Confirms whether acceptance criteria are met  
- Provides user-focused feedback  
- Accepts or requests adjustments  

#### 6. **Lead the Problem-Solving in Retrospectives**
PMs contribute by identifying:

- Requirement clarity issues  
- Communication gaps  
- Planning/scope problems  
- Workflow inefficiencies  

#### 7. **Connect Stakeholders and the Team**
PMs communicate regularly with:

- Clients  
- Executives  
- Designers  
- QA  
- Marketing  
- Engineering leads  

They translate business needs into actionable technical work.
